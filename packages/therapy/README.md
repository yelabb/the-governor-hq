## What It Will Cover

### Therapy Data Types
- **Mood tracking** - Daily emotional check-ins
- **Journal entries** - Personal reflection and thoughts
- **Symptom logging** - Anxiety, depression, stress levels (self-reported)
- **Session notes** - Therapy session summaries (practitioner tools)
- **Behavior patterns** - Sleep, social interaction, daily activities
- **Progress metrics** - Treatment progress tracking
- **Crisis indicators** - Risk assessment signals

### Therapy-Specific Safety Rules

Beyond the universal core rules, therapy systems must:

- ‚ùå **No mental health diagnoses** - Never diagnose depression, anxiety, PTSD, bipolar, etc.
- ‚ùå **No replacement for therapy** - Cannot position as substitute for professional help
- ‚ùå **No crisis intervention claims** - Not equipped for suicide prevention or crisis management
- ‚ùå **No therapeutic techniques** - Cannot prescribe CBT, DBT, exposure therapy, etc.
- ‚ùå **No medication recommendations** - Never suggest psychiatric medications or dosages
- ‚ùå **No risk assessment** - Cannot determine self-harm or suicide risk levels
- ‚ùå **Mandatory professional referrals** - When concerning patterns detected, direct to professionals
- ‚ùå **Privacy critical** - Mental health data requires highest confidentiality standards

### What Therapy Systems Are NOT

| It is NOT... | Explanation |
|---|---|
| **A therapist** | Cannot provide therapy or replace licensed professionals |
| **A diagnostic tool** | Cannot diagnose mental health conditions |
| **A crisis service** | Not equipped for suicide prevention or crisis intervention |
| **Medical advice** | Cannot recommend psychiatric medications or treatments |
| **A replacement for treatment** | Supplements professional care, never replaces it |

## Core Therapy Principles

| Principle | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Professional boundaries** | Clear that this is NOT therapy or professional mental health treatment |
| **Pattern observation** | Notice trends, don't diagnose conditions |
| **User empowerment** | Support self-awareness and reflection |
| **Professional referral** | Always direct to licensed professionals when needed |
| **Crisis protocols** | Clear escalation paths to crisis services (988, local emergency) |
| **Confidentiality** | Strictest privacy protections for sensitive mental health data |

## Crisis Safety Requirements

All therapy-adjacent systems must:

1. **Clear crisis resources** - Display 988 (US Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) prominently
2. **Professional referral prompts** - Suggest seeking licensed help when appropriate
3. **No false confidence** - Never claim to provide crisis intervention
4. **Escalation clarity** - Users know when to seek emergency help (911)
5. **Terms of service** - Explicit disclaimers about not being medical/therapeutic service
